Average Rent for Condos in Montreal

  • Around $5,500 - $10,000 - In 2024, luxury condos are still impacted by high demand, especially in urban centers and high-demand areas. Prices have remained elevated due to inflation, supply chain disruptions affecting construction, and an ongoing shortage of available high-end properties. Technology integration within units, such as smart home systems and energy-efficient features, also adds to the cost. Additionally, luxury amenities such as concierge services, pools, gyms, and communal spaces remain in high demand, driving up rent prices.

  • Around $4,500 - $8,500 - The pandemic's impact on the economy started to stabilize in 2022, but the effects were still felt. Luxury condos, particularly those in prime locations, saw rent prices climb back as people returned to cities. Remote work trends influenced the types of amenities in demand, with more space for home offices, which pushed up rental costs. The cost of living was also rising due to inflation, and construction material prices were still high, leading to fewer new luxury condos on the market.

  • Around $3,800 - $6,500 - In 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic drastically altered the real estate market. There was a brief dip in luxury condo rent prices as demand dropped due to economic uncertainty and people moving away from dense urban areas. However, premium properties in desirable locations, with amenities like home offices and outdoor spaces, still commanded high rents. Many luxury buildings adjusted their services and features to cater to new trends like remote working, virtual amenities, and socially distanced communal areas, but rent still reflected a decline in overall demand.
